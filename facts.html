Two Shows, Two Movies, and Two Video Games

Zombie Movies

Train To Busan

"Train to Busan" is a South Korean action-horror film released in 2016. Directed by Yeon Sang-ho, the movie follows the story of Seok-woo, a divorced fund manager, and his daughter, Su-an. To fulfill Su-an's wish to visit her mother in Busan, Seok-woo reluctantly boards a high-speed train from Seoul to Busan. However, as the journey progresses, a mysterious viral outbreak begins to spread across South Korea, turning people into aggressive zombies. Trapped on the train with a diverse group of passengers, including Sang-hwa, a tough and resourceful working-class man, and his pregnant wife, Seok-woo must navigate the rapidly deteriorating situation to ensure the survival of himself and his daughter. As the infection spreads and chaos ensues, the passengers must confront not only the flesh-eating zombies but also the dark sides of human nature as they fight for their lives and their humanity. "Train to Busan" is praised for its intense action sequences, well-developed characters, and social commentary on issues such as class disparity and sacrifice. It's a gripping and emotional rollercoaster that keeps viewers on the edge of their seats until the very end.

World War Z

"World War Z" is a 2013 American apocalyptic action horror film directed by Marc Forster and starring Brad Pitt. The movie is loosely based on the 2006 novel of the same name by Max Brooks. The film follows Gerry Lane, a former United Nations investigator who is living a quiet life with his family. The tranquility is abruptly disrupted when a mysterious and highly contagious virus breaks out, turning those infected into aggressive zombies. The outbreak spreads rapidly, causing massive panic and chaos worldwide. Summoned back into service to help stop the pandemic, Gerry embarks on a perilous journey across the globe, seeking information and solutions to prevent the downfall of humanity. His quest leads him to various countries, where he encounters different survival strategies and learns more about the nature of the zombie virus. Throughout his mission, Gerry faces numerous dangers, making narrow escapes from intense zombie attacks. Each step brings new challenges, including the discovery of how the zombies respond to noise and their extreme vulnerability to a particular pathogen, which eventually leads to a risky strategy to combat the spread of the virus. "World War Z" combines thrilling action with suspenseful storytelling, exploring themes of catastrophe, survival, and human resilience. It's known for its fast-paced plot, stunning visuals, and innovative depiction of zombies as a swift, swarming threat.

Zombie TV Shows

The Walking Dead

"The Walking Dead" is an American post-apocalyptic horror television series based on the comic book series of the same name by Robert Kirkman, Tony Moore, and Charlie Adlard. The show premiered on AMC in 2010 and quickly became a cultural phenomenon. The series begins with sheriff's deputy Rick Grimes waking from a coma to discover a world overrun by zombies, referred to as "walkers." He finds that the few survivors have clustered into groups with varying degrees of organization and intent, some benevolent, others malevolent. As Rick searches for his family, he encounters many survivors and becomes the reluctant leader of a group that struggles to maintain their humanity in a world where lawlessness prevails. Throughout the series, the characters face challenges not only from the undead, who are a constant threat to their safety, but also from other survivor groups competing for limited resources. Themes of leadership, morality, and the essence of humanity are explored in depth. The group's dynamics and alliances continually shift as they try to secure safe havens, gather resources, and survive against all odds. The show evolves over its seasons, exploring different settings and new characters, but the core of its narrative remains the human emotional and moral struggle in the face of the apocalypse. The relationships among the characters, the conflicts they face, and their personal growth are key focal points, making "The Walking Dead" a compelling, character-driven drama set against the backdrop of a zombie apocalypse.

Zombie Video Games

Dead Island

"Dead Island" is an action role-playing survival horror video game developed by Techland and published by Deep Silver. Released in 2011, the game is set on the fictional island of Banoi, located off the coast of Papua New Guinea. The story revolves around a group of survivors who find themselves trapped in the midst of a zombie outbreak on the island. Players assume the roles of one of four playable characters, each with their own unique abilities and skills. The game begins with the protagonist waking up in a hotel room after a night of partying, only to find that the island has been overrun by zombies. As the survivors explore the island, they encounter various other survivors, some of whom offer quests and assistance, while others present additional challenges and dangers. Throughout the game, players must scavenge for weapons, supplies, and resources to defend themselves against the hordes of undead and complete objectives. The island is divided into different areas, including a beach resort, a city, and a jungle, each with its own distinct environments and hazards. In addition to the main storyline, "Dead Island" features a cooperative multiplayer mode that allows players to team up with friends to tackle missions and challenges together. The game also includes role-playing elements such as character progression, skill trees, and crafting, allowing players to customize their experience and playstyle. "Dead Island" received praise for its immersive open-world environment, engaging gameplay, and cooperative multiplayer mechanics. However, it also faced criticism for technical issues, including bugs and glitches, as well as its portrayal of violence and gore. Overall, it remains a popular title among fans of the survival horror genre.

The Walking Dead

"The Walking Dead" video game is an episodic adventure game developed and published by Telltale Games, released between 2012 and 2019. The game is based on the comic book series of the same name by Robert Kirkman and follows a new group of characters navigating a post-apocalyptic world overrun by zombies, referred to as "walkers." The game is divided into multiple episodes, each following a different segment of the story. Players primarily control Lee Everett, a former university professor and convicted murderer, who becomes the guardian of a young girl named Clementine after the outbreak of the zombie apocalypse. The storyline focuses heavily on character development, player choices, and moral dilemmas. Throughout the game, players must make difficult decisions that impact the narrative and relationships with other characters. These choices often involve matters of life and death, as well as ethical questions about trust, loyalty, and survival. As Lee and Clementine journey through the zombie-infested world, they encounter various other survivors, both friendly and hostile, and must navigate dangerous situations while searching for safety and a sense of belonging. Along the way, they form close bonds with certain characters and face heartbreaking losses. The gameplay of "The Walking Dead" consists primarily of interactive dialogue sequences, exploration, and puzzle-solving, with occasional action sequences and quick-time events. The game's distinctive cel-shaded art style and cinematic presentation contribute to its immersive storytelling experience. "The Walking Dead" received widespread acclaim from critics and players alike for its compelling narrative, well-developed characters, and emotional depth. It won numerous awards, including several Game of the Year accolades, and is regarded as one of the greatest video games of its genre.
